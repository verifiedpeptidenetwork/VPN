Ask Your Doctor to Add: Thyroid Panel
Thyroid issues (hypo/hyperthyroidism) can mimic or worsen symptoms people often blame on peptides — fatigue, weight changes, mood, heart rate. If it's not already included in your panel, ask your doctor to add:
• TSH (Thyroid Stimulating Hormone)
• Free T3
• Free T4
• Thyroid Antibodies (TPO/TgAb) — if family history of thyroid disease
🍬
Ask Your Doctor to Add: Insulin Resistance Panel
Insulin resistance can drive fat gain, cravings, and energy crashes — and it's often missed on a standard panel because fasting glucose alone can look "normal." If it's not already included, ask your doctor to add:
• Fasting Insulin
• Fasting Glucose
• HbA1c (3-month average blood sugar)
• HOMA-IR (calculated from fasting glucose + insulin)
🔥
Ask Your Doctor to Add: Inflammation Panel
Chronic inflammation can affect recovery, gut health, joints, and how well your body responds to peptides. If it's not already included, ask your doctor to add:
• hs-CRP (high-sensitivity C-Reactive Protein)
• ESR (Erythrocyte Sedimentation Rate)
• Homocysteine

💊 Vitamins & Supplements
Evidence-based supplement guide — Thyroid · Insulin Resistance · PCOS · Inflammation · Reflux · Protein/EAA · Stress
⚠ FOR LEARNING ONLY — THIS IS NOT MEDICAL ADVICE. This is a general educational guide. People with thyroid disease, diabetes, low blood pressure, or who take medications should discuss supplements with their physician, because some supplements can alter medications or blood sugar.

Work with your doctor — even if they disagree with you — and independently verify everything in every part of this website (or any website) before acting on it.
🦋 Thyroid Support — Male & Female
Selenium
Dose: 100–200 mcg daily (do not exceed 400 mcg/day — selenium toxicity risk)
• Supports conversion of T4 to active T3
• Important for thyroid antioxidant defense
• May reduce thyroid antibodies in autoimmune thyroiditis
Zinc
Dose: 15–30 mg daily with food (above 40mg long-term may require copper monitoring)
• Supports thyroid hormone production and immune function
Vitamin D3
Dose: Based on blood levels — common maintenance 1,000–4,000 IU daily
• Supports immune regulation
• Deficiency is associated with autoimmune thyroid disorders
Omega-3 Fish Oil
Dose: 1–2g daily of combined EPA + DHA
• Helps lower inflammatory markers
• Supports cardiovascular health
🍬 Insulin Resistance — Male & Female
Berberine
Dose: 500mg, 2–3x daily with meals (total 1,000–1,500mg/day)
• Improves insulin sensitivity
• May reduce fasting glucose and HbA1c
• Activates AMPK, similar to some pathways affected by metformin
⚠ Caution: Can lower blood sugar — use caution with diabetes medications.
Myo-Inositol + D-Chiro-Inositol
Dose: Myo-Inositol 2g twice daily (4g/day total) + D-Chiro-Inositol 50mg twice daily (100mg/day total) — best researched ratio is 40:1
• Improves insulin signaling
• Supports ovulation and hormone balance in women with PCOS
• May improve metabolic markers
🌸 PCOS — Women
Myo-Inositol + D-Chiro-Inositol
Dose: 4g Myo-Inositol + 100mg D-Chiro-Inositol daily, 40:1 ratio
• Improved insulin resistance
• More regular menstrual cycles
• Improved ovulation and fertility markers
Omega-3 Fish Oil
Dose: 1–2g EPA + DHA daily
• May reduce inflammation and improve triglycerides
Vitamin D
Dose: Based on blood testing
• Deficiency is common in PCOS — correcting it may improve insulin sensitivity
🔥 Inflammation
Extra Virgin Olive Oil
Dose: 1–2 tablespoons daily
• Rich in polyphenols such as oleocanthal
• Anti-inflammatory effects similar to mild COX inhibition
• Core component of the Mediterranean diet
• Use high quality extra virgin olive oil
Curcumin with Piperine
Dose: 500–1,000mg daily of standardized curcuminoids
• Reduces inflammatory pathways including NF-κB
⚠ Caution: May interact with blood thinners.
Black Seed Oil (Nigella sativa)
Dose: 500mg twice daily, or ~1–2.5mL daily of the oil
• May improve insulin resistance
• May reduce inflammation
• May support lipid markers
• May have antioxidant effects
⚠ Warning: May lower blood pressure and blood sugar. Avoid or use medical supervision if you already have low blood pressure, or take blood pressure / diabetes medications or insulin. Monitor BP and blood sugar carefully.
🍌 Resistant Starch — Natural GLP-1 & PYY Booster
Resistant starch acts like a fermentable fiber — your small intestine can't digest it, so it travels to the large intestine where gut bacteria ferment it. That fermentation triggers the L-cells lining your gut to release more of your body's own GLP-1 and PYY, hormones that help regulate blood sugar and appetite.
Cook & Chill (Retrograde Starch)
Cook white rice, pasta, potatoes, or sweet potatoes as normal. Let cool to room temp, then refrigerate 12–24 hours before eating. This converts digestible starch into resistant starch — it stays resistant even if reheated.
Raw / Uncooked Starch
Unmodified potato starch, green (unripe) banana flour, or high-amylose maize starch are naturally resistant in their raw form — never cook them. Stir 1–2 tablespoons into cold foods like smoothies, yogurt, or overnight oats.
⚠ Caution: Start with just 1 tablespoon daily and increase gradually — too much too fast can cause bloating or gas.

Men with Thyroid & Insulin Resistance
Morning: Vitamin D3 (based on labs) · Selenium 100–200mcg · Zinc 15–30mg · Omega-3 EPA/DHA 1–2g
Meals: Berberine 500mg, 2–3x daily
Optional: Black seed oil 500mg twice daily if BP is not low
Women with PCOS, Thyroid & Insulin Resistance
Daily: Myo-Inositol 4g + D-Chiro-Inositol 100mg (40:1) · Berberine 1,000–1,500mg with meals · Selenium 100–200mcg (if appropriate) · Vitamin D (based on labs) · Omega-3 EPA/DHA 1–2g · Extra virgin olive oil 1–2 tbsp
Optional: Black seed oil (same BP precautions)
These dosages are based on the most commonly studied ranges in human clinical trials and are not random estimates.
🌶️ Acid Reflux & Esophagus Support
For GERD/acid reflux, the goal is to reduce acid exposure, support the protective mucus layer of the esophagus, and promote healing. Supplements may provide symptom relief but do not replace evaluation for severe reflux, difficulty swallowing, unexplained weight loss, vomiting blood, or long-standing symptoms.
Alginate Raft (e.g. RefluxRaft)
• Reacts with stomach acid to form a floating gel raft that reduces reflux
• Use after meals and before bed
• Good for GERD, silent reflux (LPR), throat irritation
⚠ High-sodium formulas: use caution with uncontrolled high blood pressure, heart failure, or kidney disease. Separate from medications by at least 2 hours.
Aloe Vera Liquid (e.g. Lily of the Desert)
Dose: 1–2oz once or twice daily before meals
• May soothe irritated esophageal/stomach lining
• Contains polysaccharides that may support the mucosal barrier
⚠ Avoid non-purified aloe latex (can cause diarrhea/electrolyte disturbances). Use caution with diabetes meds, diuretics, digoxin, and blood thinners.
Slippery Elm
Dose: 400–500mg capsules, 3–4x daily, before meals/bed
• Mucilage coats throat and esophagus, providing a protective barrier
⚠ Separate from all medications/supplements by at least 2 hours. Use caution with diabetes medications.
Deglycyrrhizinated Licorice (DGL)
Dose: 380–760mg chewable, 20 minutes before meals
• Stimulates protective mucus production for heartburn, gastritis, mucosal protection
⚠ DGL has glycyrrhizin removed so it's safer than regular licorice, but use caution with BP medications, diuretics, kidney disease, or pregnancy. Avoid regular licorice root with hypertension.
Zinc Carnosine
Dose: 37.5mg twice daily with meals
• Supports stomach/intestinal mucosal healing
⚠ Long-term high zinc may reduce copper levels. Separate from certain antibiotics and thyroid medications.
Lifestyle Support
• Avoid eating 2–3 hours before lying down
• Elevate the head of the bed for nighttime reflux
• Reduce trigger foods (alcohol, large fatty meals, chocolate, mint, spicy foods)
• Weight reduction can significantly improve reflux in overweight individuals
🥩 Protein & Muscle Preservation (Especially on GLP-1s)
Best Option: Whole Food Protein
If you can reach your daily protein goal from food, additional supplements aren't necessary. Examples: chicken, fish, eggs, Greek yogurt, lean beef, cottage cheese.
If You Can't Reach Protein Goals: Whey Protein
Dose: 20–40g per serving
Good brands: Optimum Nutrition Gold Standard Whey · Transparent Labs Grass Fed Whey · Dymatize ISO100. Best used after exercise, between meals, or when appetite is low from GLP-1 medications.
Essential Amino Acids (EAA)
Use if: you can't tolerate enough protein, very low appetite, fasting but want to preserve muscle, or struggle to eat enough.
Dose: 10–15g daily, especially around training
Good brands: Kion Aminos · Transparent Labs Vitality EAA · NutraBio EAA
BCAA vs EAA
EAA is superior to BCAA. BCAA only contains leucine, isoleucine, and valine — EAA contains all nine essential amino acids required for muscle protein synthesis.
Recommendation: Food protein → Whey/protein shake → EAA → BCAA (generally unnecessary if protein/EAA intake is adequate)
Simple GLP-1 Muscle & Reflux Support Stack
Morning: Protein shake if unable to meet protein goals · Aloe vera liquid if it helps
Before meals: DGL chewable · Slippery elm
After meals / before bed: RefluxRaft alginate barrier
Evening: Magnesium glycinate for sleep · EAA if protein intake is very low or after training

Dr. Vladimir Zelenko — Early Viral Treatment Philosophy

Key principle: early action — ideally within the first 5 days — before cytokine storm develops. Always get annual bloodwork to check for deficiencies like Vitamin D. Research and verify everything yourself.

🍌 Resistant Starch — Natural GLP-1 & PYY Booster
Resistant starch acts like a fermentable fiber — your small intestine can't digest it, so it travels to the large intestine where gut bacteria ferment it. That fermentation triggers the L-cells lining your gut to release more of your body's own GLP-1 and PYY, hormones that help regulate blood sugar and appetite.
How to make it at home
1. Cook & Chill (Retrograde Starch): Cook white rice, pasta, potatoes, or sweet potatoes as normal. Let cool to room temp, then refrigerate for 12–24 hours before eating. This converts digestible starch into resistant starch — it stays resistant even if reheated.
2. Raw/Uncooked Starch: Unmodified potato starch, green (unripe) banana flour, or high-amylose maize starch are naturally resistant in their raw form — never cook them. Stir 1–2 tablespoons into cold foods like smoothies, yogurt, or overnight oats.
⚠ Start with just 1 tablespoon daily and increase gradually — too much too fast can cause bloating or gas.

Database Setup
Step 1 — Create Free Supabase Account
1. Go to supabase.com → Sign up free
2. Create a new project (name it anything)
3. Wait ~2 minutes for it to provision
Step 2 — Create the Reports Table
In Supabase → SQL Editor → paste and run this:
create table reports ( id uuid default gen_random_uuid() primary key, created_at timestamptz default now(), vendor_id text, peptide text, test_type text, file_name text, file_data text, lab text, batch text, notes text ); alter table reports enable row level security; create policy "Public read" on reports for select using (true); create policy "Public insert" on reports for insert with check (true);
Step 3 — Get Your Keys
In Supabase → Project Settings → API:
Copy your Project URL
Copy your anon public key
Step 4 — Paste Keys into the HTML File
Open your index.html in a text editor
Find these two lines near the top of the script:

const SUPA_URL = '';
const SUPA_KEY = '';

Paste your URL and key between the quotes, save, and re-upload to Netlify.
